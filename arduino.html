About Arduino

Posted by Lac Nham blog on December 19, 2020

My project will mostly base on 2 fields of technology to support the project. The first is AI and Machine Learning, and the second is Arduino.

I never knew what Arduino is until I did a project in high school. Arduino is a very useful tool that is widely used in the world, and it is becoming more popular now in Viet Nam. The birth of Arduino is a great help for students who have passion for electronics, and who are studying technology and electronics.

Arduino was launched worldwide in 2005 as a tool for students to study and do research by Professor Massimo Banzi. Arduino then went viral and was widely used at breakneck speed thanks only to the good reviews of the first users.

Arduino is a platform on which every hardware device is perfect. Users just need to create ideas, prepare all the things they need and reassemble them, then they will have a complete product. Each with the symbol "Arduino" refers to pre-written lines of code developed by the Arduino community. Your job is to take that available resource and refine it according to your own needs.

The Arduino platform is quite popular for beginners in the technology and electronics field.

The Arduino structure includes: power, pins, reset button, power indicator LED, RX TX LED, IC (integrated circuit), voltage regulation.

Some products of Arduino at the moment is: Arduino Uno, Lilypad Arduino, RedBoard, Arduino Mega, Arduino Leonardo

An Arduino includes:

- Hardware: programmable microcontroller, which is expandable and can be connected with other circuits.

- The integrated IDE development software allows users to write programs in C, C ++ or uses to compile code, ..

Arduino has become a phenomenon in the electronic playground in the world thanks to its convenience and simplicity. Products from the Arduino community, which have been popular with many users, are varied in so much aspects with reasonable prices, so they are quite suitable for many requirements.

Connection

Arduino can connect and interact with many surrounding systems and environments:

- An arduino can work independently.

- Sensor system (measuring temperature, humidity, object color, ..). For example: pH meter

- Display devices (LCD display, LED light, ..)

- Modules that support wired or wireless connection to link with other devices (3G, Wifi, Bluetooth, ..)

- GPS, SMS

Some applications of Arduino in life:

- Project development for users, such as students.

- Control traffic lights, make LEDs blink on billboards.

- Make 3D printers

- Make a toaster to signal users when the cake is done.

- Control light and sound sensors (for example, I used an Arduino for fire alarm system in my project).

With a playing field and the number of people using Arduino as many as the present day, I am sure that the future of Arduino-labeled products will become brighter with higher qualities. As someone who has already used Arduino in most of my projects, I appreciates Arduino very much. It is simple and enables my projects to be more understandable and more flexible to be developed and completed.
